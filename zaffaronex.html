The Mirzaei Legacy

A Family Dedicated

In 1850, Hakim Abdul Rehman Mirzaei first planted crocus sativus in the fertile soils of the Kashmir valley. He discovered that harvesting before the break of full dawn — in the brief window between the third prayer and sunrise — produced threads of exceptional color and potency.

This discovery, passed through six generations, is the secret protocol behind every package of Naksh Zafran. No machine has ever touched our stigmas. No artificial color has ever graced our packaging.

Today, the sixth generation of the Mirzaei family continues the tradition — bringing the finest saffron directly to Pakistani homes, with the same unwavering commitment to excellence that began 170 years ago.

Daily Use Guide

How to Use Naksh Zafran

01

Golden Milk (Kesar Doodh)

Steep 10–15 threads in 2 tbsp warm (not boiling) water for 20 minutes. Add to warm full-cream milk with cardamom, honey and a pinch of turmeric. Drink before bed. Traditional remedy for sleep, skin, and immunity.

02

Biriyani & Pulao

Dissolve 20–25 threads in ¼ cup warm water. Add to rice 10 minutes before plating for authentic golden color and fragrance. A single pinch of Naksh Zafran transforms ordinary pulao into a regal dish.

03

Therapeutic Tea

Steep 8–10 threads with green tea, ginger, and honey for an anti-inflammatory morning ritual. Drink daily for mood elevation, reduced anxiety, and improved focus throughout the day.

04

Desserts (Kheer, Halwa)

Add saffron water to kheer, sheer khurma, or gajar halwa for unparalleled aroma and a beautiful saffron hue. Traditional Pakistani sweets elevated to their highest expression.
